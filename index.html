We are preserving Ghana's history,
one document at a time.

For decades, accessing a public record in Ghana meant navigating a slow, paper-based process, travelling to a PRAAD office, searching through manual registers, and waiting days or even weeks for results. Government ministries delivered records in boxes. Citizens queued in reading rooms. Important documents were often difficult to trace, retrieve, or preserve.

This is more than digitization. It is a national shift toward efficient governance, institutional transparency, and modern public service delivery in Ghana.

The PRAAD Management System is transforming how Ghana preserves and accesses its public records. Built as a unified digital platform, it securely stores records from across government institutions, makes them instantly searchable, and ensures that the right people can access the right information at the right time.

Step 1 — Create your account

Getting started takes only a few minutes. Sign up with your email address or log in using your Ghana Card where available. Government staff can also access the platform using their existing ministry credentials, making onboarding seamless across public institutions.

Step 2 — Search, upload, or connect

Citizens can easily search for and request public records from a single platform. MDA officers can upload, transfer, and manage records digitally, while partner institutions integrate directly through secure APIs. Every user only sees the records and actions they are authorized to access. Ensuring privacy, security, and compliance at every level.

Step 3 — Access what you need

Open records can be downloaded instantly, while restricted requests are reviewed and communicated directly by email. Users can track the status of every request, submission, and approval from a personalized dashboard, bringing transparency and convenience to the entire records process.
